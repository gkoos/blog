The fetch-kit ecosystem now has a dedicated website: [fetchkit.org](https://fetchkit.org).

If you've been following the tools - `@fetchkit/ffetch`, `@fetchkit/chaos-fetch`, `@fetchkit/chaos-proxy`, and the chaos arena - the site brings them all together in one place with proper overviews, usage examples, and comparison tables.

## What's on the site

- **[ffetch](https://fetchkit.org/ffetch/)** - production-ready fetch with timeouts, retries, circuit breaker, bulkhead, and typed errors
- **[chaos-fetch](https://fetchkit.org/chaos-fetch/)** - composable fetch middleware for injecting latency, failures, throttling, and mocks in tests
- **[chaos-proxy](https://fetchkit.org/chaos-proxy/)** - transport-level chaos proxy in Node.js and Go for language-agnostic testing
- **[chaos arena](https://fetchkit.org/chaos-arena/)** - browser-based benchmark for comparing fetch, axios, ky, and ffetch under controlled chaos

Each page covers what the tool does, how to use it, and when to reach for it over the alternatives.

The source is at [github.com/fetch-kit/fetch-kit.github.io](https://github.com/fetch-kit/fetch-kit.github.io).
